Blended families take a lot of work. As the father of two young boys and stepfather to three teenage girls, it’s my job to try and keep the peace. Our new family recently moved into a new home together for the first time. It’s been a pretty big adjustment for us all.

So, what was the biggest challenge we faced?

Turns out it was getting everyone the internet access they wanted.

Never underestimate the wrath of kids demanding faster internet!

Everything Sucks When the Internet is Down

The house we bought was older, but it had plenty of space for everyone. Lots of rooms.

As a modern family, each of us has access to a number of devices we use every day, including laptops, tablets, phones, PC’s, gaming stations, etc. And of course, everyone wants to log on at the same time.

Despite assurances from my internet supplier that we were getting the best package for our household, there never seemed to be enough bandwidth to keep everyone happy. And some rooms were completely void of any signal at all! No one could agree on a fixed schedule for sharing. Fights ensued. Lots of yelling.

No matter the time of day, which room I was in, or what device I was using, half the time, the internet just kept cutting out.

The wife and I had to give up watching our favorite TV series at night before bed. Too often the episode we were on would just freeze.

It got to the point where I wasn’t sure what I was paying for. It seemed my hard-earned dollars were going towards an unreliable, inconsistent, and limited connection that everybody hated.

Internet Service Providers Don’t Always Help

I couldn’t get any straight answers from my ISP. The guys I spoke to were so frustrating to deal with, always putting me on the defensive with their questions.

“What do you mean you don’t know what kind of router you have?”

“How many gigs of data do you think your wife is going to use next month?”

“What kind of gaming do your kids do?”

Seriously! Am I supposed to know all this stuff?
